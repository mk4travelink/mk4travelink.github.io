## What is KAYAK?

KAYAK is the world's leading travel search engine[reference:0]. Founded in 2004 by Steve Hafner and Paul English[reference:1], KAYAK searches hundreds of travel sites at once to show you all your options in one place[reference:2]. Whether you're looking for flights, hotels, rental cars, or vacation packages, KAYAK brings together results from airlines, hotels, online travel agencies, and car rental companies side by side[reference:3].

Part of Booking Holdings (NASDAQ: BKNG)[reference:4], KAYAK processes billions of queries across its platforms and is trusted by millions of travelers worldwide[reference:5]. The company also manages a portfolio of travel search brands including SWOODOO, checkfelix, momondo, Cheapflights, Mundi, and HotelsCombined[reference:6].

## Why Travelers Choose KAYAK

### 1. Search Everything in One Place

KAYAK is a true **all-in-one travel search engine**[reference:7]. Instead of hopping between different websites, you can compare flights, hotels, rental cars, and vacation packages in a single search[reference:8]. ### 2. AI-Powered Conversational Search

KAYAK is leading the travel industry's AI revolution with innovative tools that make trip planning faster and more intuitive.

**AI Mode**: Launched in October 2025, AI Mode is a natural-language search experience powered by ChatGPT that allows users to plan trips by typing questions in everyday language[reference:10][reference:11]. KAYAK is the first major travel site to let users plan complete trips by typing questions directly into a search box[reference:12]. Simply click the "AI Mode" icon, type travel-related questions the way you'd say them to another person, and get real-time results as KAYAK compares the latest information and prices from hundreds of travel providers[reference:13].

**Ask AI**: Building on AI Mode, KAYAK introduced Ask AI—an industry-first conversational travel planning experience that lets travelers start planning their trip in a chat while flight, hotel, and rental car results update live alongside the conversation[reference:14]. Search for hotels near a stadium, compare flights between cities, or build your full itinerary—all without switching tabs[reference:15].
